# About — Luca Caristina

My work lives at the intersection of thought, structure, technology and meaning.

I do not define myself through a single role.
My background combines scientific research, web development, publishing, artificial intelligence and complex systems design. Over time, this convergence has shaped a specific capability: the ability to take complex, often undefined ideas and transform them into structured, coherent and operational systems.

## What I Actually Do

I design systems.

Not just software, and not just concepts — but structured systems that integrate:

* technology
* processes
* content
* decision logic
* human interaction

My role is to bring clarity where there is complexity, and structure where there is fragmentation.

This means:

* translating abstract ideas into concrete architectures
* designing AI-assisted workflows and agentic systems
* structuring platforms and digital infrastructures
* defining information architecture and operational logic
* connecting strategy with real implementation

## How I Work

I approach every project as a system.

Before tools, before code, before execution — I work on:

* understanding the real problem
* identifying the core structure
* defining the logic of the system
* designing how different layers interact

Only after this phase, implementation becomes meaningful.

This is why my work often sits between disciplines:

* AI systems and LLM-based architectures
* web development and platform design
* complex systems and multi-scale thinking
* Web3 and decentralized infrastructures
* strategic writing and content architecture

## Focus Areas

My current work is focused on:

* AI-driven systems and agentic workflows
* custom LLM-based project design (local and cloud)
* orchestration of parallel agents and processes
* AI-assisted decision and support systems
* multi-scale platforms and observability concepts
* integration of AI layers into existing infrastructures
* Web3 systems with real utility (not speculative models)
